Choosing the Right Fiber Made Simple

Macro, micro, and steel fibers each solve different problems in concrete performance. Here is how to choose the right one.

Macro Fiber
Steel replacement for high-demand slabs and floors.
  • Steel replacement in slabs and floors
  • Higher toughness and crack control
  • Built for durability at scale
Best for: slabs, industrial floors, pavements, infrastructure
Steel Fiber
Maximum load-bearing capacity and structural strength.
  • Maximum structural capacity
  • Impact and fatigue resistance
  • Extreme performance environments
Best for: runways, tunnels, heavy-duty industrial projects
Micro Fiber
Plastic shrinkage crack control and surface integrity.
  • Plastic shrinkage crack control
  • Improved surface integrity
  • Uniform distribution in mixes
Best for: plaster, screed, precast, finishing layers

Why Replace Steel Reinforcement with Fiber

Fiber-reinforced concrete improves performance, reduces cost, and simplifies construction compared to traditional steel reinforcement.

Crack Control & Durability
  • Improved shrinkage crack control
  • Higher toughness under service loads

Corrosion-Free Performance
  • No corrosion-driven deterioration
  • Consistent performance over time
  • Lower maintenance exposure
Simplified Construction
  • Cleaner workflows with fewer constraints
  • Less congestion around reinforcement
  • Faster placement and finishing
Cost & Efficiency
  • Reduced labor and handling time
  • Fewer installation steps on site
  • Better schedule reliability

How Fiber Reinforcement Works

A simplified concrete reinforcement workflow using GTS fiber systems.

  1. 01

    Add Fiber

    Add GTS Fiber directly into the concrete mix during batching.

  2. 02

    Mix Thoroughly

    Mix the concrete thoroughly to ensure even fiber dispersion throughout the mix.

  3. 03

    Pour Concrete

    Place the fiber-reinforced concrete using standard construction methods.

  4. 04

    Level & Finish

    Level, screed, and finish the concrete surface according to project requirements.

  5. 05

    Uniform Reinforcement

    Fibers remain evenly distributed throughout the concrete to improve durability and crack control.
